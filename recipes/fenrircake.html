Fenrir cake

Description:

Beware the bite of Fenrir Cake — a deceptively sweet creation cloaked in white, hiding the wild spirit of the great wolf within. With soft, spiced layers and a luscious vanilla frosting, it is a treat forged for feasts... feared by the gods, devoured by mortals.


Ingredients:

  • 200g unsalted butter, softened – churned by shieldmaidens
  • 200g sugar – sweetness stolen from the gods
  • 3 large eggs – heart of the beast
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract – song of the Valkyries
  • 250g plain flour – dust from the fields of Midgard
  • 150g cream cheese – soft bite of Fenrir
  • 120ml milk – white as snow in Jotunheim

Forging the Fenrir Cake:

  1. Raise the Beast
    2. Cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy — the howl before the storm. Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla.
  2. Mix the Foundation
    3. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, cinnamon, cardamom (or nutmeg), and salt. Add to the wet mix in parts, alternating with milk, until smooth.
  3. Bake the Form
    4. Pour into a greased 20cm round cake tin. Bake at 175°C (350°F) for 25–30 minutes, until golden and firm like the chains that held Fenrir. Let it cool completely.
  4. Whip the Frost
    5. Beat cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Add vanilla, then sift in powdered sugar and beat until the frosting is thick like a winter blizzard.
  5. Unleash and Adorn
    6. Frost the cooled cake generously. Swirl the top like a snowstorm over a battlefield. Optionally, decorate with shards of toasted nuts or silver sprinkles — the teeth of Fenrir.
